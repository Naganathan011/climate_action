The Crisis

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While some of these shifts are natural, since the Industrial Revolution, human activities have been the primary driver — burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes that release massive amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

The consequences are profound: rising sea levels, intensifying storms, melting ice caps, and disrupted ecosystems. Without urgent action, global average temperatures could rise by more than 3°C above pre-industrial levels by 2100 — an outcome with catastrophic consequences for life on Earth.

Root Causes

Why Is This Happening?

Human Activities

Industrialization, transportation, and agriculture collectively account for over 70% of global greenhouse gas emissions each year.

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

CO₂, methane, and nitrous oxide trap solar radiation, steadily raising Earth's surface temperature over decades.

Global Warming

The gradual increase in Earth's average surface temperature, primarily driven by the enhanced greenhouse effect.

Climate Imbalance

Disrupted atmospheric circulation patterns lead to erratic monsoons, prolonged droughts, and unseasonal weather worldwide.

Environmental Impact

Deforestation reduces carbon sinks; soil degradation and ocean acidification compound the damage at an ecosystem level.

Threat to Life

Millions of species face extinction as habitats shift faster than ecosystems can adapt — including human communities.

Consequences

Effects & Challenges

Rising Temperatures

Record heat waves are occurring more frequently, threatening agriculture, human health, and urban infrastructure.

Melting Glaciers

Arctic and Antarctic ice sheets are retreating at alarming rates, with glacier loss accelerating every decade.

Extreme Weather

Hurricanes, cyclones, and storms are becoming stronger and more unpredictable due to warmer ocean temperatures.

Sea Level Rise

Coastal cities and island nations face inundation as global sea levels rise by an estimated 1–3 metres this century.

Biodiversity Loss

Over one million species are threatened with extinction as their habitats change faster than they can adapt.

The Way Forward

Solutions & Proposed Ideas

Renewable Energy Transition

Replacing fossil fuels with solar, wind, and hydro power can reduce energy-sector emissions by up to 85% — the single most impactful lever we have.

Afforestation & Reforestation

Planting billions of trees globally can sequester billions of tonnes of CO₂ while restoring critical ecosystems.

Energy Efficiency

Smart buildings, LED lighting, and efficient appliances can cut energy use by 30–40% with existing technology.

Smart Technology: IoT & AI

Intelligent grids, AI-optimised supply chains, and IoT sensors dramatically reduce waste and energy consumption.

Awareness & Policy

Carbon pricing, international agreements, and public education campaigns drive systemic behavioural and legislative change.
